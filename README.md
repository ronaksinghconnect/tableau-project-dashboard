# Netflix Movies & TV Shows Dashboard – Data Analytics Project

## Overview
This project presents an interactive dashboard analyzing Netflix’s catalog of movies and TV shows using a real-world dataset sourced from Kaggle.

The analysis focuses on understanding **content distribution, ratings, genres, geographic reach, and growth trends over time**.  
The dataset spans content released and added to Netflix between **2008 and 2020**, capturing Netflix’s major global expansion phase.

This project demonstrates:
- Exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- Business-focused insights
- Clear data storytelling through visuals
- Practical dashboard design for stakeholders

---

## Data Source
- **Source:** Kaggle  
- **Dataset:** Netflix Movies and TV Shows  
- **Time period covered:** 2008 – 2020  
- **Dataset link:** https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/shivamb/netflix-shows  

The dataset is publicly available and used strictly for analytical and educational purposes.

---

## Dataset Description
The dataset contains metadata for Netflix titles, including:
- Title
- Type (Movie or TV Show)
- Release year
- Date added to Netflix
- Duration (minutes or seasons)
- Rating (TV-MA, TV-14, etc.)
- Genre
- Country
- Description

---

## Dashboard Components & Insights

### Title-Level Overview
A detailed snapshot of a selected title showing:
- Type (Movie / TV Show)
- Release year
- Duration
- Rating
- Genre
- Date added
- Description

This allows quick inspection of individual content without scanning the entire dataset.

---

### Total Movies & TV Shows by Country
A world map visualizing Netflix content distribution by country.

Key insights:
- The United States dominates Netflix’s content library
- Strong representation from India, the UK, Canada, and European countries
- Reflects Netflix’s global content acquisition strategy

---

### Ratings Distribution
A bar chart showing the number of titles by rating.

Key insights:
- TV-MA and TV-14 dominate Netflix’s catalog
- Indicates a strong focus on mature and teen audiences
- Relatively limited children-only content

---

### Movies vs TV Shows Distribution
A comparative visualization showing:
- Movies: ~68%
- TV Shows: ~32%

Insight:
Netflix prioritizes movies, while TV shows support long-term user engagement.

---

### Top 10 Genres
A horizontal bar chart displaying the most frequent genres.

Key insights:
- Documentaries and Stand-Up Comedy lead the catalog
- High volume of Drama and International Movies
- Family and Kids content has a consistent but smaller presence

---

### Total Movies & TV Shows by Year (2008–2020)
A time-series visualization showing content additions over time.

Key insights:
- Minimal content before 2013
- Rapid growth after 2015
- Peak additions during 2018–2019
- Slight decline toward 2020, likely influenced by market saturation and external factors

---

## Key Takeaways
- Netflix’s catalog is movie-heavy with a strong global footprint
- Mature-rated content dominates the platform
- Documentaries and international dramas are key content pillars
- Netflix experienced rapid catalog expansion between 2015 and 2019
